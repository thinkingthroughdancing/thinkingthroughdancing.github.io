OUR TEAM

HANNAH

Hannah is a sophomore at NYU majoring in Social Studies Education with a minor in Global and Urban Education and hopes to one day teach high school US History. Hannah is currently working on the curriculum board for TTD, is a member of NYU Alternative Breaks, and serving as Corresponding Secretary on the Chapter Council of her sorority, Kappa Kappa Gamma. Growing up Hannah studied ballet at Boston Ballet, and performed in their production of the Nutcracker as both an angel and a soldier. While it's been some time since she's been on the dance floor, she is eager to see students learn and grow through dance as she did through TTD's programming.

ERICA YU

Erica is the secretary and head of administrative department for TTD. She is currently an NYU undergraduate double majoring in French and Global Liberal Studies with a concentration of politics, rights and development. She has had a lot of hand-on experiences with administrative and legislative work with her past internships in Ningbo Municipal People’s Government, Ningbo TV Station and Beijing Denton’s Law firm. She was also an English tutor with students aging from 10 to 20 years old. She is trained to work with students and cope with problems.

LINDA WANG

Linda is the marketing director of TTD. I am always in search of new experiences, and I accept challenges as problems that can be solved once given enough thinking. I aspire to inspire, help and mentor those around me. I am also interested in the intersection between art, media and communications. I have worked as a PR intern at MSLGroup, an affiliated PR agency of Publicis Groupe, where I had a chance to use strategic communication skills to increase vitality for different brands, including Electrolux, Abbott and Allergan etc. I also interned at the marketing department of Glow Recipe, a Korean beauty company, where I gained video editing and copy writing skills. On the other hand, I am a professional pianist with more than 15 years of piano playing experience. I believe music and art can help young teenagers to improve critical thinking skills.

WENDY HOU

Wendy, from China, is Media, Culture, and Communication major student at New York University. After joining TTD in January 2017, Wendy has been working with the dance curriculum team to create the boot camp TTD offers in the summers. She has been able to contribute to the team with her 12-year professional training in Chinese traditional and minority-group dancing and knowledge on contemporary, hip-hop, and Bollywood dance styles. Besides TTD, Wendy was in NYU Nasha dance team in freshmen year and performs for Chinese Students and Scholars Association annual galas. Currently, she is also an intern for Pentacle, a major dance company in America, where she learns about education for art and leadership in the dance industry.

ALLIE

Allie is a Manhattan-based filmmaker who grew up with a camcorder in one hand and ballet slippers in the other. She was heavily influenced by her exposure to the arts at a very young age -- from modern dance, tap, and ballet, to theater and even live television. For Allie, art is more than just an extra-curricuar -- it is a way to learn about yourself and the world, and express complicated emotions and ideas. Through her work with TTD, Allie is combining her love of filmmaking with her desire to share the arts with others.
